Aviation training is **expensive** both in terms of **time and money**. Expensive
for schools to run training, expensive for instructors to develop, improve
and assess training and expensive for students to take part in training.

I believe there is a lot of learning from modern **Open Source software development**
that can be applied to aviation for the **benefit of students, instructors and
training organisations** as well as for **improved pilot safety** more generally.

## Why Open Aviation Solutions exists

Open Aviation Solutions exists to **accelerate** the use of **free and open source**:
- [interactive learning components](/learning-components/) that can be used and improved by instructors or students;
- **interactive lesson presentations** that can be used, adapted and improved by instructors and schools;
- **software libraries** that can be used and improved by schools or other organisations, making it cheaper to build
  the next tool for aviation.

Read more about Open Aviation Solutions' [free and open source licensing](/licensing/).

Open Aviation Solutions also aims to facilitate other **cheaper (but not free) learning
experiences** for students through:

- cheaper **consumer-grade virtual reality simulator setups** which students can learn to use
  effectively in school and **gradually build their own** at home for a life-long
  pilot training resource;
- cheaper, more efficient, modern software for use in student training, built
  with the above open source software libraries (this is a longer-term goal).

I started Open Aviation Solutions as a way to combine three of my passions:

I plan to sustain Open Aviation Solutions through paid contracts or sponsorships to:
- develop new or customised [interactive learning components](/learning-components/);
- customise learning resources for organisations (such as tailoring a set of
  interactive RPL briefing presentations to your organisational needs, or
  using a custom 3D model of your actual training aircraft in an interactive
  component);
- develop custom software for aviation organisations;
- deliver custom setups and training for more technical simulator learning
  strategies (such as integrating Virtual Air Traffic into your students'
  simulator skills).
